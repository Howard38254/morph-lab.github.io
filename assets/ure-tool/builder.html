Apprentice Research Builder FAQ

How do I add blocks?

Open the Library panel (Toggle Library), then drag a block card onto the canvas. Alternatively, double-click an empty area of the canvas or use the "Add Blank Block" button to create a new block at a default position.

How do I connect blocks with arrows?

Double-click the outer edge of a block to start an arrow from that block. Then single-click the outer edge of a different block to complete the connection — the arrow will point from the first block toward the second. Press Esc, left-click empty space, or right-click to cancel. Arrows stay anchored to their blocks when you move them. You can also double-click the label at an arrow's midpoint to add or edit text.

How do I edit a block?

Double-click any block on the canvas to open the Inspector — a floating, draggable panel that appears at the top-right of the workspace. From there you can edit the block's title, short and long descriptions, notes, and video URL, as well as duplicate or delete the block. Click "Save" in the inspector to apply your changes.

How do I expand a block's full description?

Blocks show a short description by default. Click the expand toggle on a block to reveal its full long description, which includes objectives, time needed, and procedure details. Click again to collapse back to the short view.

How do I resize blocks?

Drag the grip indicator in the bottom-right corner of any block. Text and spacing inside the block scale proportionally as you resize. Dimensions are saved and persist across sessions.

How do I delete blocks or connections?

Select a block and press Delete or Backspace, or open the Inspector and click "Delete." To remove an arrow, left-click on it.

How do I use the zoom controls?

Use Ctrl+scroll over the canvas to zoom in and out (range: 25%–150%). The toolbar above the canvas also provides −/+ buttons and a fit-to-view (⊡) button that centers and scales the canvas to fit your workspace. Your zoom level is saved to local storage.

How do I save and load layouts?

Click "Save" in the header to download your current layout as a JSON file. Click "Load" to import a previously saved file. The Notes panel also autosaves its content to local storage.

How do I undo actions?

Click the "Undo" button in the header toolbar to step back through recent actions such as moves, edits, deletions, and connections. In addition, you can use Ctrl+Z.

What do the side panels do?

Mentorship Plan (left panel): A free-form text area pre-populated with research mentorship planning prompts. Content autosaves to your browser's local storage.
Library (right panel): A categorized catalog of block templates representing Early Exposure activities (blue) and Student Research Training Workshops (teal). Browse or search, then drag blocks onto the canvas to use them.

What do the EM indicator chips mean?

Each block displays small colored chips indicating its alignment with Entrepreneurial Mindset dimensions: C (Curiosity, yellow), Co (Connections, purple), and V (Creating Value, green). These help you see at a glance which EM competencies each activity develops.
